# Project Claim Guardrails

Use this file for the project:

`ERP Microstate Dynamics of Inhibitory Control in First-Grade Children With Attention, Reading, and Mathematics Risk`

The purpose is to keep interpretation and manuscript claims proportional to the evidence.

## Central Paper Logic

Risk profile -> Go/No-Go task demand -> ERP/microstate temporal dynamics -> developmental interpretation -> relation to attention, reading, and mathematics risk.

Keep the paper focused on how risk-related individual differences are associated with inhibitory-control-related neural dynamics. Do not let the argument drift into general diagnosis, source localization, or broad cognitive ability claims unless directly supported.

## Preferred Framing

Prefer:

- "risk indicators" over "disorders" unless diagnostic criteria were used;
- "associated with" over "caused by";
- "consistent with" over "demonstrates";
- "scalp topography" over "brain region" unless source analysis was performed;
- "microstate configuration" over "neural module";
- "inhibitory-control-related processing" over "pure inhibition" unless the task and behavior isolate inhibition.

## Claims That Need Strong Evidence

Require direct behavioral or literature support before claiming:

- a group has impaired inhibitory control;
- a microstate represents response inhibition;
- posterior maps indicate compensatory processing;
- reading or math risk causes altered executive control;
- a scalp pattern reflects a specific cortical source;
- group differences are diagnostic markers.

## Common Reviewer Risks

- Groups are unbalanced or defined by arbitrary cutoffs.
- Continuous risk scores are reduced to categories without justification.
- Go/NoGo effects may reflect attention, visual processing, or salience rather than inhibition.
- ERP windows are chosen after inspecting significant effects.
- Microstate K is selected because it gives an interpretable result rather than by fit/stability criteria.
- Grand averages hide individual variability.
- Results are topographic but interpreted as source-level mechanisms.
- Multiple comparisons across maps, windows, groups, and scores are not controlled.

## Recommended Paper Strategy

- Use continuous attention, reading, and math scores as the primary explanatory variables when feasible.
- Use categorical risk groups for visualization, descriptive comparison, and robustness checks.
- Report behavioral performance alongside ERP/microstate results.
- Treat non-adult-like posterior patterns as a developmental finding, not automatically as an error.
- If using individual evokeds for clustering, justify it as preserving subject-level variability and supporting dimensional analysis.
- If also using grand-average segmentation, present it as a comparability or robustness analysis.

## Safe Discussion Pattern

```text
The present findings suggest that first-grade children's Go/No-Go processing was characterized by [observed pattern], with [risk/score] associated with [microstate/ERP metric]. This pattern is compatible with [mechanism], but the inference should be constrained by [task/method limitation]. Because Go/No-Go performance depends on attention, stimulus evaluation, response preparation, and inhibition, the result is best interpreted as altered inhibitory-control-related processing rather than a pure inhibition deficit. Future analyses linking microstate metrics to commission errors, RT variability, and dimensional risk scores would strengthen this interpretation.
```
