# Ethics And Interpretation Boundaries

Use this file when work involves children, clinical labels, education-risk categories, sensitive human data, diagnosis, prediction, or high-impact decisions.

## Child And Developmental Data

- Avoid deficit-only language.
- Do not imply stable individual ability from a single task or neural measure.
- Distinguish group-level research findings from individual diagnosis.
- State developmental variability and measurement limitations.
- Avoid stigmatizing labels when the study uses risk-screening rather than clinical diagnosis.

## Clinical And Risk Labels

Use clinical labels only when the sample was clinically diagnosed by appropriate criteria.

Prefer:

- attention-risk children;
- children with elevated ADHD symptoms;
- children at reading risk;
- children with mathematics difficulties;
- children showing task-related differences.

Avoid:

- "ADHD children" for non-diagnostic attention-risk groups;
- "dyslexic" or "dyscalculic" when only screening risk is available;
- "impaired brain function" without strong clinical evidence;
- "biomarker" unless validated independently.

## Neural Interpretation Boundaries

Do not infer:

- brain source from scalp topography alone;
- causal mechanism from correlation;
- diagnosis from EEG/ERP/fMRI pattern;
- educational intervention target from one exploratory result;
- domain-general cognitive deficit from one task.

Use cautious language:

- may reflect;
- is consistent with;
- provides indirect evidence;
- suggests a possible;
- should be interpreted cautiously.

## Privacy And Data

- Do not upload participant data to external services unless the user explicitly confirms it is allowed and anonymized.
- Do not expose personal identifiers, school identifiers, contact details, dates of birth, or raw clinical records.
- When sharing a skill or workflow, exclude PDFs, manuscripts, results, data, and local paths unless they are intentionally public.

## Grant/Application Claims

Keep claims proportional:

- separate completed work from proposed work;
- distinguish preliminary evidence from established findings;
- state risks and mitigation;
- avoid promising diagnostic or intervention impact without validation.

## Reviewer-Facing Boundary Statement

When needed, add a limitation sentence:

> Because the present data are group-level and task-specific, the observed neural differences should be interpreted as evidence of task-related processing dynamics rather than individual diagnostic markers.
