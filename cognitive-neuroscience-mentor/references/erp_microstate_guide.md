# ERP And Microstate Guide

## Inhibitory Control And Go/No-Go

Do not treat Go/No-Go as a pure measure of inhibition. It usually mixes:

- response inhibition;
- sustained attention;
- response preparation;
- conflict or salience processing;
- error monitoring;
- speed-accuracy tradeoff;
- motivation and task engagement.

Interpret child data developmentally. Immature or different ERP/microstate dynamics may reflect delayed maturation, compensatory recruitment, attentional instability, or task-strategy differences.

## Traditional ERP Components

N2 is often discussed in relation to conflict monitoring, inhibition, or cognitive control, but its interpretation depends on task, age, electrodes, and condition contrast.

P3/P300 is often discussed in relation to attentional resource allocation, context updating, evaluation, or inhibition-related processing in No-Go tasks. Avoid saying it directly equals inhibition.

When linking microstates to components, check:

- time window overlap;
- topographic similarity;
- condition sensitivity;
- behavioral correlation;
- whether the component interpretation is supported by child literature.

## ERP Microstate Interpretation

An ERP microstate is a stable topographic pattern over a short time interval. It should usually be interpreted as a temporally specific processing stage, not as a one-to-one marker of a cognitive function.

Common metrics:

- duration: how long a topography remains dominant;
- coverage: proportion of time or data explained by a map;
- GEV: global explained variance;
- onset/offset: timing of a state;
- transition: sequence probability between maps;
- map strength or GFP-related measures when used.

Interpretation checklist:

1. What time window does the map occupy?
2. Which condition or group changes it?
3. Is the map topography similar to known ERP components or previous maps?
4. Is the finding linked to behavior?
5. Are trial counts and signal quality adequate?
6. Is the cluster number justified?
7. Is the group comparison based on fitting the same maps?

## Developmental And Risk-Group Caution

Attention risk, reading risk, and mathematics risk should not be collapsed into the same mechanism.

- Attention risk: consider sustained attention, response monitoring, inhibition failures, P3/resource allocation, ADHD-related indirect evidence.
- Reading risk: separate domain-general executive function from reading/language-specific processing.
- Mathematics risk: consider executive function, working memory, attention, numerical processing, and domain-general learning risk. Search externally because local math-risk evidence may be thin.

Use clinical ADHD, dyslexia, or dyscalculia literature cautiously when the user's sample is non-diagnostic risk-group children.
