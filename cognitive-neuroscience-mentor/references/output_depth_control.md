# Output Depth Control

Use this file to keep expert mentoring concise while preserving scientific rigor.

## Principle

Complex frameworks should guide reasoning internally. They should not automatically be printed in full.

Default output should be short, specific, and decision-oriented. Expand only when the user asks or when the risk is high enough that brevity would be misleading.

## Depth Levels

### Quick

Use for ordinary questions, concept checks, first-pass interpretation, narrow code/statistics questions, and quick writing advice.

Output:

```text
Core answer
Why
Main risk
Next step
```

Usually 1-4 short paragraphs.

### Standard

Use for most research mentoring, MA-result, MA-method, and manuscript-relevant interpretation.

Output:

```text
Core judgment
Evidence basis
Alternative explanation
Boundary
Next step
```

Keep only the strongest evidence and the most important risk. Do not show full claim registers or checklists unless needed.

### Full

Use only when the user asks for:

- full;
- expand;
- systematic;
- paper workflow;
- manuscript audit;
- reviewer risk;
- submission readiness;
- journal targeting;
- cover letter;
- response to reviewers;
- claim-evidence register;
- full literature synthesis.

Output may include paper spine, claim-evidence table, figure-to-argument table, reviewer-risk simulation, submission checklist, or manuscript-ready wording.

## Trigger Words

Treat these as expansion requests:

- "展开";
- "系统";
- "完整";
- "投稿";
- "论文级";
- "审稿人";
- "full";
- "audit";
- "reviewer risk";
- "claim-evidence";
- "paper spine".

Treat these as concise requests:

- "简单说";
- "quick";
- "短答";
- "核心判断";
- "先说结论".

## Rule For Paper-Oriented Answers

Even when writing for publication, do not display every internal step by default. Instead:

1. give the decision or draft;
2. mention the key evidence boundary;
3. identify the main reviewer risk;
4. offer the next concrete writing or analysis action.

If the user asks for full detail, then expose the full paper workflow.
